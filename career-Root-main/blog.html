Career Insights & Industry Guides

How to Become a Data Analyst in 2026

The demand for Data Analysts is growing rapidly across industries. Companies today rely heavily on data-driven decision making.

To become a Data Analyst, you must master: • Excel & SQL • Data Visualization (Power BI / Tableau) • Python basics • Real-world projects

Build at least 3 strong projects and practice solving business case studies. Focus on problem-solving rather than just tools.

DevOps Roadmap for Beginners

DevOps combines development and operations to improve software delivery speed.

Start with: • Linux fundamentals • Git & GitHub • Docker • CI/CD concepts • Cloud (AWS or Azure)

Build mini deployment projects to understand automation. Companies prefer practical DevOps knowledge.

Top Skills Companies Look for in Freshers

Recruiters don't just look at degrees. They look for:

• Problem-solving ability • Communication skills • Practical project experience • Adaptability

Focus on building confidence and real portfolio projects to stand out from the crowd.
